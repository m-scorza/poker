# 10 — Poker Glossary

Compiled from *Dicionário do Poker* (Reg Life) and supplemented from the Estudar dá Sorte series. [Glossary]

---

## A

- **Action**: any play (bet, raise, call, check, fold)
- **Add-on**: buying extra chips in rebuy tournaments (usually at break)
- **Aggressive**: player who bets/raises frequently
- **All-in**: betting all remaining chips
- **Ante**: mandatory bet from all players before dealing

## B

- **Backdoor**: draw requiring two consecutive cards (turn + river) to complete
- **Bad beat**: losing with a strong hand to an unlikely draw
- **Bankroll**: total money reserved for poker
- **Barrel**: a bet on a street (double barrel = bet flop + turn; triple barrel = bet all three streets)
- **Big blind (BB)**: largest mandatory pre-flop bet; also the position
- **Blank**: a card that doesn't change the board texture
- **Blocker**: holding a card that reduces the combos of hands your opponent can have
- **Bluff**: betting with a weak hand to make opponents fold better hands
- **Bluff-catcher**: a hand that beats bluffs but loses to value bets
- **Board**: the community cards (flop + turn + river)
- **Bounty**: reward for eliminating a player (in KO/PKO tournaments)
- **Broadway**: any card T, J, Q, K, A; a broadway straight = TJQKA
- **Bubble**: the point just before money positions (e.g., 31 left with 30 paying)
- **Bubble factor**: multiplier representing ICM pressure at the bubble
- **Burn card**: card discarded before dealing each street
- **Button (BTN)**: dealer position; last to act post-flop; best seat at the table
- **Buy-in**: entry fee for a tournament or cash game minimum

## C

- **Call**: matching the current bet
- **C-bet (Continuation bet)**: betting the flop after being the pre-flop aggressor
- **Check**: passing action without betting (only when no bet is pending)
- **Check-raise**: checking then raising when opponent bets
- **Chip EV (cEV)**: expected value measured in chips (ignoring ICM)
- **Chip leader (CL)**: player with the most chips
- **Combo / Combination**: specific card combination (e.g., A♠K♠ is one combo of AKs)
- **Condensed range**: range of only medium-strength hands
- **Cooler**: unavoidable clash of two very strong hands
- **Cutoff (CO)**: position to the right of the button

## D

- **Dead money**: chips in the pot from players who have folded
- **Deep stack**: having many big blinds (typically 50bb+)
- **Donk bet**: leading into the pre-flop aggressor (out of turn with initiative)
- **Donkey**: weak/recreational player (pejorative)
- **Double barrel**: betting both flop and turn
- **Draw / Drawing hand**: a hand needing more cards to become strong
- **Dry board**: board with few draws (e.g., K-7-2 rainbow)

## E

- **Early position (EP)**: UTG, UTG+1 — first to act pre-flop
- **Effective stack**: the smaller stack between two players in a hand
- **Equity**: percentage chance of winning at showdown
- **Equity realization (EQR)**: factor of how much crude equity you actually capture
- **EV (Expected Value)**: average long-term result of a decision
- **Exploit**: deviating from GTO to take advantage of opponent's mistakes

## F

- **Field**: all players in a tournament
- **Fish**: weak/recreational player
- **Flat (call)**: calling a raise without re-raising
- **Float**: calling a bet with intention to take the pot away later
- **Flop**: first three community cards
- **Flush**: five cards of the same suit
- **Flush draw**: four cards to a flush
- **Fold**: discarding your hand; giving up
- **Fold equity**: expected frequency that your bet will make opponents fold
- **Four-bet (4-bet)**: the fourth raise in a pre-flop sequence (open → 3-bet → 4-bet)
- **Freeroll**: tournament with no entry fee but real prizes
- **Freezeout**: tournament with no rebuys or add-ons
- **Full house**: three of a kind + a pair

## G

- **Grind**: playing consistently over long sessions
- **GTO (Game Theory Optimal)**: theoretically perfect, unexploitable strategy
- **Gutshot**: inside straight draw (4 outs)

## H

- **Hand history**: record of all actions in a hand
- **Heads-up (HU)**: one-on-one play (final two players or a dedicated format)
- **Hero**: you (the protagonist in a hand analysis)
- **HiJack (HJ)**: position two seats right of the button
- **HUD (Heads-Up Display)**: real-time statistical overlay on poker tables

## I

- **ICM (Independent Chip Model)**: model converting chip stacks to monetary value
- **Implied odds**: potential future winnings factored into current pot odds
- **In position (IP)**: acting after your opponent post-flop
- **ITM (In The Money)**: finishing in a paid position

## K

- **Knockout (KO)**: tournament format where eliminating a player earns a fixed bounty

## L

- **LAG (Loose Aggressive)**: playing many hands aggressively
- **Late position**: CO, BTN — last to act pre-flop (advantageous)
- **Leak**: systematic error in your game
- **Limp**: calling the big blind pre-flop instead of raising
- **Linear range**: range of consecutively ranked hands with no gaps
- **LoJack (LJ)**: position three seats right of the button

## M

- **MDA (Mass Data Analysis)**: analyzing aggregate data from all opponents
- **Min-raise**: raising the minimum allowed amount (2× the big blind)
- **MTT (Multi-Table Tournament)**: tournament with multiple tables
- **Multiway**: pot with 3+ players

## N

- **Nit**: extremely tight player who only plays premium hands
- **Nodelock**: solver feature that locks one player's strategy to model exploits
- **Nuts**: the best possible hand given the board

## O

- **OESD (Open-Ended Straight Draw)**: straight draw with 8 outs
- **Off-suit (o)**: two hole cards of different suits
- **Open raise / Open**: the first voluntary raise pre-flop
- **Out of position (OOP)**: acting before your opponent post-flop
- **Outs**: cards remaining in the deck that improve your hand
- **Overbet**: betting more than the pot size
- **Overcall**: calling after another player has already called
- **Overpair**: pocket pair higher than any card on the board

## P

- **Passive**: player who mostly checks and calls (rarely bets/raises)
- **Pay jump**: increase in prize money between finishing positions
- **PKO (Progressive Knockout)**: bounty grows each time a player eliminates someone
- **Polarized range**: range of only strong hands and bluffs, no middle
- **Position**: seat relative to the dealer button
- **Pot odds**: ratio of the bet to call vs. the total pot
- **Probe bet**: leading the turn after a flop check-check
- **PSKO**: Progressive Super Knockout (variant of PKO)
- **Push/Fold**: strategy reduced to all-in or fold (typically < 10bb)

## R

- **Rainbow**: board with all different suits (no flush draws)
- **Range**: the set of all possible hands a player could hold
- **Range advantage**: having more equity than opponent on a given board
- **Rebuy**: buying back into a tournament after busting
- **Reg (Regular)**: frequent/professional player
- **Resteal**: 3-betting a late-position open raise to steal the pot
- **RFI (Raise First In)**: being the first to raise pre-flop
- **Risk advantage**: having lower risk premium than opponent (ICM concept)
- **Risk premium (RP)**: extra equity required to compensate for ICM pressure
- **River**: fifth and final community card
- **Robust equity**: equity that holds up as opponent's range strengthens

## S

- **Satellite**: tournament where the prize is a seat in a larger tournament
- **Semi-bluff**: betting with a drawing hand that can improve
- **Shove**: going all-in
- **Showdown**: revealing hands to determine the winner
- **Sit & Go (SNG)**: tournament that starts when seats are filled
- **Sizing**: the amount of a bet or raise
- **Solver**: software that calculates GTO solutions
- **SPR (Stack-to-Pot Ratio)**: effective stack divided by pot size
- **Squeeze**: 3-betting after an open raise and at least one call
- **SRP (Single Raised Pot)**: pot with only one pre-flop raise
- **Stack**: total chips a player has
- **Steal**: open raising from late position to win the blinds
- **Suited (s)**: two hole cards of the same suit

## T

- **TAG (Tight Aggressive)**: tight hand selection with aggressive betting
- **Tell**: physical or behavioral clue about hand strength
- **Thin value bet**: value bet with a marginally strong hand
- **Tight**: playing a narrow range of hands
- **Tilt**: making irrational decisions due to emotional frustration
- **Top pair**: pair made with the highest card on the board
- **Trap**: playing passively with a strong hand to disguise its strength
- **Trips**: three of a kind using two board cards and one hole card
- **Turn**: fourth community card

## V

- **Value bet**: betting to get called by worse hands
- **Variance**: natural fluctuation of results around the expected value
- **Villain**: your opponent in a hand analysis
- **VPIP (Voluntarily Put Money In Pot)**: % of hands where you voluntarily enter the pot

## W

- **Walk**: everyone folds to the big blind (BB wins without playing)
- **Wet board**: board with many draws (e.g., 8♠7♠6♥)
- **Winrate**: profitability metric (earnings per N hands/tournaments)
- **WSOP (World Series of Poker)**: the most prestigious poker tournament series
